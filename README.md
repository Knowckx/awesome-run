### 🔥 2025年技术移民新风向

**核心总结**：
*   **英语国家（美加澳英）**：**难度史诗级提升**。不再是"有个计算机学位就能润"的时代，拼的是**高薪、高级职位、STEM特定领域（如AI/工程）**。普通码农（Devs）分数线极高。
*   **非英语国家（日德）**：目前仍是政策红利期。不仅降低学历/语言门槛，还推出了"无Offer找工作"签证。


#### 1. AU 澳大利亚：高不可攀
- **189独立技术移民**：软件工程师 (Software Engineer) 这类热门IT职业的获邀分数普遍飙升至 **90-100分**。
    *   *这意味着什么？* 雅思8炸(20分) + 年龄满分(30分) + 学历(15分) + 单身/配偶(10分) + 工作经验(5-10分) 几乎要全部拉满。
- **485毕业生工签（重大削减）**：
    *   年龄上限从50岁断崖式降至 **35岁**（研究型硕士/博士除外）。这直接切断了很多大龄留学转码的路。
    *   时长缩短，不再享有以前的"超长待机"。

#### 2. 🇨🇦 加拿大：收紧
- **大幅削减配额**：2025年永久居民目标下调至 **39.5万**（原定50万）。
- **工签收紧**：毕业工签 (PGWP) 更难拿，部分专业不再符合资格；配偶工签限制增多。
- **临时居民上限**：首次设立临时居民（留学/工签）上限，意在减少总人数。


#### 3. 🇬🇧 英国：门槛大涨
- **工签年薪门槛暴涨**：2024/2025周期，`Skilled Worker Visa` 的基础门槛涨至 **£38,700**（较之前大幅上涨）。
    *   初级开发 (Junior Dev) 的薪资往往达不到这个标准，导致企业很难为应届生或初级人员提供Sponsorship。

#### 4. 🇩🇪 德国：无需学位也能润，机会最大
*   **机会卡 (Chancenkarte)**：**无需德国Offer**，凭分数（满6分）即可拿签证去德国找工作1年。
    *   利好IT：IT专业如果有2年以上相关工作经验，**无需大学学位**也能申请。
*   **欧盟蓝卡 (Blue Card) 放宽**：
    *   IT紧缺职业的年薪门槛降至 **€43,759.80** (2025标准)。
    *   **无学历者**：只要有3年相关IT工作经验，即可替代大学学位申请蓝卡。

#### 5. 🇯🇵 日本：顶级人才优待，普通IT门槛低
*   **J-Skip (特别高度人才)**：
    *   硕士学历 + 年收2000万日元（约100万人民币）= **1年后直接拿永住**。
    *   适合国内大厂P7/P8以上级别的精英直接空降。
*   **J-Find (未来创造人才)**：
    *   **全球Top 100大学**毕业生（如清北复交浙科中等），毕业5年内，可直接申请**2年签证**去日本找工作/创业，无需Offer。
*   **普通工签**：最大门槛是日语，大部分岗位至少要有日语N2的水平。日本的工作机会多，对只有本科学历甚至大专的普通程序员依然非常友好，几乎没有配额限制，

#### 6. 🇸🇬 新加坡 (精英化)
- **EP 门槛提高**：2025年1月起，EP最低月薪门槛涨至 **$5,600**（金融业 $6,200）

### 基础知识

技术移民一般分为三个阶段  
- 工签，申请人拿到工签后就可以在这个国家合法工作。工签都没有那就是黑工，查到会被送到小黑屋遣返
- PR(Permanent Resident), 也叫永居, 绿卡。拥有后可以在这个国家长期自由居住，工作。  
大部分权利和本国公民差不多，只是因为不能换护照，法理上你还是中国公民。  
大部分国家的PR都有移民监，例如每5年需要在这个国家呆满2年，否则PR可能被取消。  
- 换国籍，完全成为那个国家的公民，可以换护照，从此你不再是中国公民，而是"外籍华人"。

### �🔥 2025 Programmer Immigration Trends (New)

**Core Summary**:
*   **English-speaking Countries (US, Canada, Australia, UK)**: **Difficulty historically increased**. It is no longer an era where "a CS degree gets you out". It now requires **high salary, senior positions, and STEM specific fields (e.g., AI/Engineering)**. Invitation scores for general developers are extremely high.
*   **Non-English-speaking Countries (Japan, Germany)**: **Still in a policy bonus period**. Not only are degree/language thresholds lowered, but "Job Seeker" visas (no offer required) have also been introduced.

#### 1. 🇦🇺 Australia: Out of Reach
- **189 Skilled Independent Visa**: Invitation scores for popular IT occupations like Software Engineers have soared to **90-100 points**.
    *   *What does this mean?* IELTS 8 (20 pts) + Max Age (30 pts) + Degree (15 pts) + Single/Partner (10 pts) + Work Experience (5-10 pts) — you almost need to max out everything.
- **485 Temporary Graduate Visa (Major Cut)**:
    *   Age limit dropped precipitously from 50 to **35 years old** (except for research masters/PhD). This cuts off the path for many mature-age students pivoting to IT.
    *   Duration shortened; no longer enjoying the previous "extended stay".

#### 2. 🇨🇦 Canada: Tightening
- **Quota Slashed**: 2025 permanent resident target lowered to **395,000** (originally 500,000).
- **Work Permit Tightening**: PGWP is harder to get, some majors no longer qualify; Spousal Open Work Permits have more restrictions.
- **Temporary Resident Cap**: First-time cap on temporary residents (students/workers), aiming to reduce total numbers.

#### 3. 🇬🇧 UK: Threshold Surge
- **Work Visa Salary Threshold Skyrocketed**: For the 2024/2025 cycle, the base threshold for `Skilled Worker Visa` rose to **£38,700** (a significant increase).
    *   Salaries for Junior Devs often fail to meet this standard, making it hard for companies to sponsor fresh graduates or juniors.

#### 4. 🇩🇪 Germany: No Degree Needed, Biggest Opportunity
*   **Opportunity Card (Chancenkarte)**: **No German Offer needed**. You can get a 1-year visa to find work in Germany based on points (6 pts).
    *   *Benefit for IT*: IT specialists with 2+ years of relevant experience can apply **without a university degree**.
*   **EU Blue Card Relaxed**:
    *   Salary threshold for IT shortage occupations lowered to **€43,759.80** (2025 standard).
    *   **No Degree**: 3 years of relevant IT experience can substitute for a university degree to apply for a Blue Card.

#### 5. 🇯🇵 Japan: Top Talent Privileges, Low Threshold for General IT
*   **J-Skip (Special Highly Skilled Professional)**:
    *   Master's degree + Annual income of 20 million JPY (~1M RMB) = **PR in 1 year**.
    *   Suitable for elites (e.g., P7/P8 level in big tech) to parachute in directly.
*   **J-Find (Future Creation Individual)**:
    *   **Global Top 100 University** graduates (graduated within 5 years) can apply for a **2-year visa** to find work/start a business in Japan, no Offer required.
*   **General Work Visa**: The biggest threshold is Japanese language (usually N2 level). There are many job opportunities, and it is very friendly to ordinary programmers with only a bachelor's or even associate degree, with almost no quota limits.

#### 6. 🇸🇬 Singapore (Elitism)
- **EP Threshold Raised**: From Jan 1, 2025, the minimum monthly salary for Employment Pass (EP) rose to **S$5,600** (Financial sector S$6,200).


### Basic Knowledge

Technical immigration generally involves three stages:
- **Work Visa**: Allows the applicant to work legally in the country. Without a work visa, you are working illegally and risk deportation if caught.
- **PR (Permanent Resident)**: Also known as a Green Card. Allows long-term free residence and work in the country. Most rights are similar to citizens, but you cannot hold a passport, and legally you are still a Chinese citizen. Most PRs have residency obligations ("immigration jail"), e.g., living 2 years every 5 years to maintain status.
- **Citizenship (Naturalization)**: Becoming a full citizen of that country, obtaining a passport, and ceasing to be a Chinese citizen (becoming a "foreign Chinese").
