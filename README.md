# English

This is a curated Awesome list of global mobility paths and immigration strategies for tech talents.


In an increasingly interconnected world, opportunities for tech professionals are no longer confined by borders. Whether you are seeking new career challenges, a different lifestyle, or exploring global opportunities, access to clear and accurate information is crucial for making informed decisions.  

The name "run" is a nod to a Chinese internet meme (润, rùn), symbolizing the act of seeking new horizons. This project, awesome-run, aims to provide a clear, concise, and up-to-date comparison of permanent residency and citizenship pathways in various countries, designed primarily for programmers, software engineers, and other tech professionals.  

Immigration policies are complex and change frequently. The strength of this guide comes from the community. We enthusiastically welcome your contributions! If you spot an error, have an update on a policy, or want to add a new country to the list, please feel free to open a Pull Request or an Issue.  

**Disclaimer**: The information provided here is for reference purposes only and should not be considered legal or immigration advice. Always consult the official government immigration websites for the most accurate and up-to-date information before making any decisions.



### **Global Paths to Permanent Residency and Citizenship for Tech Professionals**

| Region | Country/Region | Path to Permanent Residency (PR) | Residency Obligation (to Maintain PR) | Path to Citizenship / Passport | Dual Citizenship |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Asia** | **Hong Kong** | **Right of Abode (Permanent Resident Status)**<br>- Achieved after 7 years of continuous ordinary residence in Hong Kong. | For non-Chinese citizens, status may be lost if absent from Hong Kong for 36 consecutive months. | To apply for an HKSAR Passport, one must be a Chinese citizen and a Hong Kong permanent resident. | **Not recognized.**<br>However, foreigners can hold their original citizenship while being a Hong Kong permanent resident. |
| | **Japan** | **Permanent Resident (PR, 永住者)**<br>- **Standard path:** 10 years of continuous residence.<br>- **HSP Visa path:** Apply after 1 or 3 years as a Highly Skilled Professional.<br>- Requires review of tax/social security payments and a clean criminal record. | - Renew Residence Card every 7 years.<br>- A Re-entry Permit is required if leaving Japan for more than one year. | **Naturalization:**<br>- 5+ years of continuous residence.<br>- Japanese language proficiency (at least N3 level) and an interview with an immigration officer. | **Not recognized.** |
| | **Singapore** | **Permanent Resident (PR)**<br>- Apply after holding a work pass (EP/SP) for a period. Approval is discretionary with no clear point system.<br>- Typically takes several years for applicants to be approved. | - Renew Re-entry Permit (REP) every 5 years.<br>- Renewal depends on your ties to Singapore (e.g., tax payments, CPF contributions). | **Naturalization:**<br>- Apply after being a PR for at least 2 years.<br>- Also discretionary.<br>- Mandatory National Service (NS) for second-generation male citizens. | **Not recognized.** |
| **Oceania** | **Australia** | **Permanent Resident (PR)**<br>- Via the points-based General Skilled Migration program (visas 189/190/491). Prepare documents, submit an Expression of Interest (EOI), and wait in the pool.<br>- If selected, you receive an invitation to apply. PR can be granted directly, even if you've never been to or worked in Australia.<br>- Highly competitive. Waiting time for an invitation depends heavily on your occupation and points score, ranging from months to several years. | **Residency Obligation:** Must live in Australia for 2 out of every 5 years. | - As a PR, must have lived in Australia for 3 out of the last 4 years (incl. 9 months in the final year).<br>- Pass a citizenship test (in English). | **Recognized.** |
| | **New Zealand** | **Permanent Resident Visa (PRV)**<br>- **Core:** Secure a skilled job offer in NZ.<br>- Achieve 6 points under the Skilled Migrant Category (SMC) to apply for a Resident Visa (RV).<br>- After holding an RV and meeting residency requirements for 2+ years, you can apply for the PRV. | **The only truly permanent PR with no travel restrictions.** The PRV allows you to leave and return to NZ indefinitely without conditions. | - Must have lived in NZ for at least 240 days in each of the 5 years before applying.<br>- NZ citizens have rights to live and work in Australia indefinitely. | **Recognized.** |
| **North<br>America** | **Canada** | **Permanent Resident (PR)**<br>- Via the federal Express Entry system or Provincial Nominee Programs (PNP).<br>- The process typically takes around 1-2 years from invitation to PR. | **Residency Obligation:** Must be physically present in Canada for at least 2 out of every 5 years. | - Must be a PR and have been physically present in Canada for at least 3 out of the last 5 years.<br>- Tax filing records are reviewed.<br>- Pass a language and citizenship test. | **Recognized.** |
| | **USA** | **Permanent Resident (Green Card)**<br>- **Typical path:** H1B visa lottery → Employer-sponsored EB-2/3.<br>- Highly uncertain with very long waiting periods (backlogs) for applicants from certain countries (e.g., China, India). | - Absence from the U.S. for >6 months may lead to scrutiny; >1 year may be considered abandonment of PR. | - After holding a Green Card for 5 years.<br>- Must have 30 months of physical presence in the U.S. during that period.<br>- Pass an English and civics test. | **Recognized.** |
